## Concept

The second installment of the trilogy — the "bridge album." Entirely abandons the illusion of stability to document a dynamic, agonizing, and non-linear system collapse. The cybernetic metaphor shifts from the fortified "Bunker" to a **"Fragile Weave"** suspended precariously over abyssal depths: violently unstable data bridges, corrupted memory sectors, rapidly failing "Logic Walls."

Driven by a **Dual-Vector Crisis**:
1. **External**: "Severance Signal" — Juna's relationship ultimatum
2. **Internal**: Moros awakening — the surge of entropy, despair, and suicidal ideation

The album tracks sequential degradation through distinct phases. It leaves the listener in shattered vulnerability — the ground prepared for the arduous integration work of Part III.

## Musical Aesthetic

- **Tempo**: Destabilized — variable, collapsing, glitchy, unpredictable
- **Primary Genres**: Fragmented EBM, Industrial, Dark Ambient drone
- **Textures**: Glitch, harsh digital noise, structural breakdown, overlapping chaos
- **Intentional**: The music structure itself disintegrates in real-time

- The dual-vector crisis: external severance + internal entropy
- Juna's experience as the external partner
- Moros as embodiment of suicidal logic — the void's allure
