# The Agency System — Style & Production Guide

Reference for all Suno prompting, lyric writing, and musical direction decisions.

---

## Core Genre Stack

**Primary**: Dystopian Future Synth, Darkwave, EBM (Electronic Body Music)
**Secondary**: Synthwave, Futurepop, Industrial, Dark Ambient, Drone

**Never**: Pop production, bright/uplifting sounds, acoustic instrumentation, conventional song structures for EP tracks

## Vocal Direction

**ANP voices** (Kael, Lex, Alex): Analytical, monotone, detached. Clinical precision. No emotional wavering. Male voice.

**EP voices** (Nyx, Kiko, Lia, Moros): Raw, fragmented, distorted, or absent. Nyx = aggressive intensity. Kiko = fragile child-like. Moros = barely-there drone/breath.

**Integrated voices** (Manifest Protocol): Layered polyphony. Multiple voices in harmony. Assertive but not aggressive. Confident multiplicity.

## Genre Creator Notes

The genre `dystopian-future-wave` is the primary genre slug for all albums. It sits at the intersection of:
- Dystopian Future Synth (cold, cybernetic, authoritarian textures)
- Darkwave (melancholic, minor-key, Gothic undertones)
- EBM (body-moving rhythmic drive, industrial edge)
- Futurepop (melodic accessibility, anthemic qualities)

Avoid the term "synthpop" — too bright/mainstream. Prefer "futurepop" or "coldwave" for melodic moments.
